Student questions: Market failure in the financial sector

1. Missing words- from: moral … illiquid … customer … imperfect … asymmetric

The 2007-2009 financial crisis revealed various market failures in finance. Key financial markets such as exchange rates, interest rates and the bond market were being systematically rigged to enable banks and bankers to make super-normal profits (and then claim their bonuses). In many cases the market-rigging was possible due to asymmetric information: market participants knew what was going on, but external clients did not. The cynical approach of market traders was encouraged by huge financial incentives to make short-term profits; the cynicism was perhaps enhanced by moral hazard, as bankers may have felt sure that their employers were ‘too big to fail’, i.e. the state (taxpayers) would step in to bail out illiquid or even insolvent banks.

2. State aid for UK Banks


2.1 Explain why a government might give £billions in financial support for struggling banks.

In such a globalized world, financial firms are greatly interconnected. This means that the failure of one firm may cause other firms to fail. Thus, the problem of TBTF may extend to financial contagion, meaning that there may be runs on other banks. Alternatively, the Central Bank may have to intervene as the Lender of Last Resort, to prevent liquidity problems.
